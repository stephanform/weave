## Conference Concept

  

### Vision

  

Europe is undergoing multiple, simultaneous transformations, ecological, digital, social, cultural, and institutional. No single discipline, no single institution, and no single perspective can address this complexity alone. What is needed is the ability to weave together diverse threads of knowledge, practice, and collaboration into a coherent and purposeful whole.

  

Universities are uniquely positioned to take on this role. As places of research, teaching, and civic engagement, they bring together disciplines, generations, and communities. As members of collaborative alliances such as STARS EU, they can act collectively across borders, pooling expertise, sharing methodologies, and amplifying impact.

  

**WEAVE 2027** brings together researchers, practitioners, and European stakeholders to explore a central question:

  

> *How can the multiple transformations Europe is undergoing, ecological, digital, social, cultural, and institutional, be woven together into coherent pathways for a sustainable, inclusive, and dynamic future?*

  

Universities, working through alliances such as STARS EU, are well placed to host and inform this debate, but the conference focuses on the substance of transformation across sectors and disciplines, not on higher education as such.

  

---

  

### Mission

  

WEAVE 2027 places **European collaboration, interdisciplinary research, and transformative education** at the heart of the debate. Hosted by the STARS EU TIG **Innovation & Entrepreneurship** at Hochschule Bremen, the conference is open to contributions from all nine STARS EU TIGs and from researchers outside the Alliance.

  

The conference is guided by a core belief: **transformation is not linear, not singular, and not achieved alone.** It requires the weaving of many strands, of knowledge and practice, tradition and innovation, local rootedness and European vision.

### The WEAVE Metaphor

  

The image of weaving is central to the conference identity. Just as a woven fabric draws strength and beauty from the interlocking of many individual threads, **WEAVE 2027** draws its power from the connections between:

  

- **Disciplines**, natural sciences, social sciences, humanities, arts, engineering, and beyond

- **Institutions**, the partner universities of the STARS EU Alliance and beyond, each bringing distinct strengths and perspectives

- **Transformation pathways**, the nine TIGs, each addressing a different dimension of European change

- **Actors**, researchers, educators, students, practitioners, policy makers, and communities

  

The result is not a patchwork, but a **fabric**, coherent, resilient, and greater than the sum of its parts.

### Format and Structure

  

WEAVE 2027 is designed as a **1.5-day international conference** for up to 60 participants, hosted in two parallel rooms at Hochschule Bremen on **Wednesday 21 and Thursday 22 April 2027**, with an opening Networking Dinner on Tuesday 20 April. Each 90-minute session slot hosts three papers, each paper is presented in 15 to 18 minutes, followed by a 7 to 8 minute Discussant statement and 4 to 5 minutes of audience Q&A. Each accepted paper is assigned one Discussant, drawn reciprocally from the pool of accepted authors.

  

**Tuesday evening, 20 April 2027, Welcome and Networking**

- 18:00 Welcome Reception at HSB

- 19:00 Networking Dinner

  

**Wednesday, 21 April 2027, full day, Laying the Threads**

- 08:30–09:00 Registration and morning coffee

- 09:00–09:30 Opening Ceremony, welcome from HSB and STARS EU

- 09:30–10:30 Keynote 1: Multiple Transformation and the European University

- 10:30–11:00 Coffee break

- 11:00–12:30 Parallel Sessions A1 and B1

- 12:30–13:30 Lunch

- 13:30–15:00 Parallel Sessions A2 and B2

- 15:00–15:30 Coffee break

- 15:30–17:00 Parallel Sessions A3 and B3

- 17:00–18:30 Cross-TIG Open Space

- 19:00 Conference Dinner

  

**Thursday, 22 April 2027, half day, Weaving the Fabric**

- 09:00–10:30 Parallel Sessions A4 and B4

- 10:30–11:00 Coffee break

- 11:00–12:00 Keynote 2: Collaboration as a Transformative Force

- 12:00–12:30 Closing Plenary and WEAVE Declaration

- 12:30–13:30 Farewell Lunch

  

This baseline accommodates up to 24 papers on Day 1 and 6 papers on Day 2, for a total of 30 paper slots. If submission volume warrants, a third parallel room can be added, raising capacity to 45 slots.

  

**Cross-TIG Open Space.** The 90-minute block on Wednesday afternoon is kept free of paper presentations. One parallel room runs an open cross-TIG discussion format; the second parallel room is available on request to any STARS EU TIG whose coordinators wish to hold a TIG meeting during the conference. No TIG is pre-assigned the room, the offer goes equally to all nine, and the scientific programme stays clearly dominant.

  

**Online sessions.** Alongside the on-site programme, WEAVE 2027 runs parallel online-only sessions delivered by video conference. These are not hybrid sessions, each session is either fully on-site or fully online, never mixed. Online sessions follow the same 90-minute format, the same reciprocal Discussant model, and the same review standard as on-site sessions, and are peer-moderated by the participating authors. They add presentation capacity without requiring a third physical room and allow researchers who cannot travel to take part. The Opening Ceremony, both Keynotes, and the Closing Plenary are streamed so that online participants share the common moments of the conference.

### Thematic Focus

  

WEAVE 2027 is open to contributions from all nine STARS EU Thematic Interest Groups on an equal footing. Submissions are not pre-sorted into tracks. The Scientific Committee allocates accepted papers to thematic sessions after review, drawing on the natural connections between the TIGs.

  

Particularly welcome are contributions that **weave across two or more TIGs**, examining how different transformation pathways intersect in research, teaching, or practice.

  

---

  

### Submission Format

  

A single submission format applies, the **Extended Abstract** of 800 to 1 500 words. The submission must include:

  

- Research question and motivation

- Theoretical framing

- Method or approach

- Preliminary or expected findings

- Contribution to the conference theme

  

Upon acceptance, authors may optionally upload a **Full Paper** of up to 8 000 words for distribution to the assigned Discussant and posting on the conference website. The Full Paper is not a publication and is not peer-reviewed beyond the Discussant exchange.

  

WEAVE 2027 does not publish conference proceedings. Authors are encouraged to submit their work to scientific journals after the conference. The Programme Committee will explore a Special Issue arrangement with one or more journal partners.

  

All submissions must be in English and follow the conference template available on the conference website.

  

Each presenting author may submit at most two Extended Abstracts to WEAVE 2027. There is no limit on co-authorship of further submissions. At submission, authors indicate whether they intend to present on-site or in a parallel online session.

  

---

  

### Discussant Format

  

Each accepted paper is assigned a Discussant. Discussants are drawn reciprocally from the pool of accepted authors, meaning each presenter is expected to discuss one paper from another author. Discussants receive the Full Paper, or the Extended Abstract if no Full Paper is uploaded, three weeks before the conference and prepare a 7 to 8 minute statement covering contribution, limitations, and suggestions for development. This peer-driven format ensures scholarly quality at no additional cost.

  

Online and on-site sessions each use this reciprocal model within their own mode, so that an online presenter discusses an online paper and an on-site presenter discusses an on-site paper.

  

---

  

### Participation Modes

  

WEAVE 2027 is held on-site at Hochschule Bremen and, in parallel, runs online-only sessions for authors who cannot travel. The two modes are not hybrid, each session is either fully on-site or fully online, never mixed. Both modes are equal in standing, the same double-blind review, the same Extended Abstract format, and the same reciprocal Discussant model apply, and online and on-site papers are reviewed together against the same standard.

  

Authors indicate their preferred mode at submission. Online participation is offered at a reduced conference fee, reflecting that catering and the networking programme are limited to on-site attendance. Fee details are published when registration opens.

## Working Notes, Research Center Engagement

  

*Working area, not part of the public Call for Papers. Captured 2026-05-20 for further development with Stephan Form and eventual refinement into the internal CfP outreach plan.*

  

**Origin.** A contact at IPB Bragança suggested engaging the research centers of the STARS EU partner universities before the CfP opens. Research centers could help in two ways, by forwarding the CfP to suitable colleagues and by possibly funding the travel of their own researchers.

  

**Two distinct asks.** Dissemination and travel funding work differently and should be handled separately.

  

### Dissemination through research centers

  

A research center is a living organisational unit with a director, internal seminars, a newsletter, and its own mailing lists, unlike a TIG mailing list that reaches scattered individuals. When a center director forwards the CfP, it carries an implicit endorsement, which is worth more than plain advertising.

  

The external CfP (`WEAVE_CfP_external.tex`) is the instrument for this, a public two-page document. It should be sent to center contacts with a short personal cover note, not on its own, since the director will want to know what is being endorsed.

  

### Funding through research centers

  

The intended ask is narrow. A research center should consider covering the **travel and participation costs of its own researchers**. This is the cleanest form of support. It directly addresses the main barrier named in the CfP, travel at the participants' own expense, while no external money enters the WEAVE budget, no co-branding is owed, and the STARS EU principle of equal footing is untouched. This is the only funding route WEAVE actively encourages.

  

**The Scopus constraint.** Center outreach must be honest on one point from the outset. WEAVE 2027 deliberately publishes no conference proceedings. As a broad, cross-TIG primer conference it will have neither proceedings nor a Scopus or comparable indexing, and that is by design, not a shortcoming. Some research centers, particularly in business disciplines with strict bibliometric funding rules, fund attendance only at conferences whose proceedings are Scopus-ranked. Where that rule is rigid, the center cannot support WEAVE at all, and outreach should say so plainly rather than let it surface late. Where the rule governs conference sponsorship but not ordinary staff travel or development budgets, the travel route may still be open. Center contacts are best placed to judge which case applies, so the proceedings situation should be stated early and without euphemism.

  

**Direct contributions to the WEAVE budget are not solicited.** Asking a center to pay into the conference budget is not part of the outreach. It invites the Scopus question in its sharpest form, raises co-branding and equal-footing problems. The HSB call itself does not stand in the way here: its ceiling is a cap on the STARS EU grant, not on the total conference budget, and the call neither limits other revenue such as conference fees nor forbids external co-funding. The reason to refrain is strategic, not a compliance constraint. If a center offers a budget contribution unprompted, that is a separate matter, to be handled case by case with Stephan Form and, on the HSB-call side, with Katrin Winkler. The open points would then be what the center receives in return, at most a flat and uniform "Supporting Institutions" acknowledgement equal for all centers, and the risk that a single co-branding center undermines the equal footing of the nine TIGs. Reserved slots, programme influence, and keynote places are never offered, since they conflict with the double-blind review.

  

### Practical considerations

  

- **Timing.** Now, during the application phase. The CfP opens 2026-09-01, so center outreach should run before that. Expressed interest from a research center is a stronger, more institutional signal than an individual commitment and supports the HSB application directly.

- **Bragança as pilot.** The IPB Bragança contact is warm and already on the bilateral contact list in the internal CfP. He can introduce the relevant IPB research center. If the pattern works, it can be replicated with the other partners.

- **Prioritisation.** Nine universities with several centers each cannot all be covered. Focus on centers aligned with the four TIGs already identified as closest to Innovation & Entrepreneurship in the internal CfP, namely Sustainable Industry, Digital Transformation, Circular Economy, and Inclusion & Social Justice, plus the universities with an existing personal contact.

- **Scientific Committee.** Center directors and senior researchers are natural candidates for the Scientific Committee, which is still to be constituted. Center outreach surfaces these people as a by-product.

  

### Boundary

  

The external CfP is for dissemination to potential authors. It is not a sponsorship prospectus and should not be turned into one. The suggestion that a center fund its own researchers' travel belongs in a separate, short partner note to center directors, kept clearly apart from the CfP itself. In the internal CfP, the research center dimension is essentially a sharpening of the existing bilateral outreach channel.
