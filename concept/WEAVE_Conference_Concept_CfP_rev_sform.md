## Conference Concept

### Vision

Europe is undergoing multiple, simultaneous transformations – ecological, digital, social, cultural, and institutional. No single discipline, no single institution, and no single perspective can address this complexity alone. What is needed is the ability to weave together diverse threads of knowledge, practice, and collaboration into a coherent and purposeful whole.

Universities are uniquely positioned to take on this role. As places of research, teaching, and civic engagement, they bring together disciplines, generations, and communities. As members of collaborative alliances such as STARS EU, they can act collectively across borders – pooling expertise, sharing methodologies, and amplifying impact.

**WEAVE 2026** brings together academics, researchers, educators, students, and European stakeholders to explore a central question:

> *How can European universities – collaborating across disciplines, institutions, and borders – weave together multiple transformation pathways into a shared vision for a sustainable, inclusive, and dynamic Europe?*

---

### Mission

WEAVE 2026 places **European collaboration, interdisciplinary research, and transformative education** at the heart of the debate. It examines how universities, working together through the nine Thematic Interest Groups (TIGs) of the STARS EU consortium, can respond to the pressing challenges of multiple transformation – not in isolation, but as a collective, connected force.

The conference is guided by a core belief: **transformation is not linear, not singular, and not achieved alone.** It requires the weaving of many strands – knowledge and practice, tradition and innovation, local rootedness and European vision.

---

### The STARS EU Thematic Interest Groups (TIGs)

WEAVE 2026 is structured around the nine TIGs of the STARS EU Alliance. Each TIG represents a distinct strand of transformation – and together they form the fabric of the conference:

| TIG | Transformation Strand |
|-----|-----------------------|
| **Energy Transition** | Towards carbon-neutral societies: collaborative research, curricula, and community engagement |
| **Art & Creative Industries** | Cultural transformation: creativity, identity, and the evolving role of arts in European societies |
| **Entrepreneurship & Innovation** | Economic transformation: university-driven ecosystems, start-up cultures, and innovation pedagogy |
| **Sustainable Industry** | Industrial transformation: circular production, responsible manufacturing, and applied research partnerships |
| **Circular Economy** | Resource transformation: systemic design, material intelligence, and lifecycle thinking in education |
| **Digital Transformation** | Technological transformation: digital literacy, data governance, and the future of the university itself |
| **Healthy Ageing** | Demographic transformation: interdisciplinary research, assistive innovation, and ageing-inclusive education |
| **Living Spaces** | Spatial transformation: smart cities, sustainable mobility, and community-engaged learning |
| **Inclusion & Social Justice** | Social transformation: equity, access, participatory research, and the ethics of change |

---

### The WEAVE Metaphor

The image of weaving is central to the conference identity. Just as a woven fabric draws strength and beauty from the interlocking of many individual threads, **WEAVE 2026** draws its power from the connections between:

- **Disciplines** – natural sciences, social sciences, humanities, arts, engineering, and beyond
- **Institutions** – nine partner universities, each bringing distinct strengths and perspectives
- **Transformation pathways** – nine TIGs, each addressing a different dimension of European change
- **Actors** – researchers, educators, students, practitioners, policy makers, and communities

The result is not a patchwork, but a **fabric** – coherent, resilient, and greater than the sum of its parts.

---

### Format & Structure

WEAVE 2026 is designed as a **two-day international conference** combining academic depth with collaborative practice.

**Day 1 – Laying the Threads**
- Opening Ceremony & Welcome by STARS EU Rector's Conference
- Keynote: Multiple Transformation and the European University – A New Paradigm
- Panel: Weaving Knowledge – Interdisciplinary Collaboration in Research and Teaching
- TIG Strand Sessions (parallel tracks, one per Thematic Interest Group)
- Poster Exhibition & Networking Reception

**Day 2 – Weaving the Fabric**
- Keynote: Collaboration as a Transformative Force – Lessons from European University Alliances
- Cross-TIG Synthesis Workshops: Where do the strands connect?
- Special Session: The Future of the STARS EU Alliance – Research Agenda and Collaborative Opportunities
- Closing Plenary & WEAVE Declaration: A Collective Commitment to Multiple Transformation
- Conference Dinner & Cultural Programme

### Invitation

We invite researchers, educators, practitioners, and doctoral candidates to submit contributions to **WEAVE 2026**, the STARS EU Alliance International Conference on Multiple Transformation.

WEAVE 2026 explores how European universities – collaborating across disciplines, institutions, and borders – can address the complex, interconnected challenges of our time. We seek contributions that examine multiple transformation processes and the role of higher education, research, and teaching in driving, shaping, and responding to change across Europe.

We welcome submissions across all nine STARS EU Thematic Interest Groups (TIGs) as well as cross-cutting contributions that weave together multiple fields and perspectives.

---

### Thematic Tracks

Submissions are invited under the following thematic tracks, corresponding to the STARS EU TIGs:

1. **Transformation & Energy Transition** – Sustainable energy research, green curricula, community energy projects
2. **Transformation & Creative Industries** – Cultural change, arts-based research, creative pedagogies
3. **Transformation & Entrepreneurship** – Innovation ecosystems, entrepreneurial education, university-industry collaboration
4. **Transformation & Sustainable Industry** – Circular production, industrial symbiosis, applied research partnerships
5. **Transformation & Circular Economy** – Systemic design, resource intelligence, lifecycle education
6. **Transformation & Digital Futures** – Digital literacy, data ethics, technology governance in higher education
7. **Transformation & Healthy Ageing** – Demographic change, assistive innovation, interdisciplinary health research
8. **Transformation & Living Spaces** – Urban resilience, smart mobility, place-based learning
9. **Transformation & Inclusion** – Equity in higher education, participatory research, social justice and access

**Cross-cutting Tracks:**

10. **Weaving Disciplines** – Interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary research methodologies and practices
11. **Weaving Institutions** – European university alliances, collaborative research cultures, and joint teaching programmes
12. **Weaving Communities** – Universities as civic actors: community engagement, co-creation, and knowledge transfer

### Submission Formats

| Format | Description | Length |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Full Paper** | Original research with methodology and findings | 4,000–6,000 words |
| **Short Paper** | Work-in-progress or conceptual contribution | 2,000–3,000 words |

| **Artistic Contribution** | Creative or performative work engaging with transformation themes | Proposal: 300–500 words |

All submissions must be in **English** and follow the conference template (available on the conference website).
